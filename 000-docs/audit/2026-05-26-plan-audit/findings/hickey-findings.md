# Rich Hickey — Plan Audit Findings — 2026-05-27

## Summary

- The plan introduces a 14th entity `SkillVersion` that is concept-overlapping with Blueprint B's existing `SkillSnapshot` (§ 2.9). The plan defends this as "NOT a duplicate" but never establishes the predicate that distinguishes them. This is the most expensive defect to leave latent: a duplicated concept in a kernel that downstream Zod / Pydantic codegen depends on.
- The "tri-linkage discipline" (AC-12 / § 3.5 PR-1 / § 5.5 verifier) stores the same identity in three plain-text places (doc body, bead body, GH issue body) and adds CI to keep them in sync. This is the textbook "place" Hickey warns about — three mutable locations holding values that must agree, with the agreement enforced by a verifier rather than by a derivation relationship.
- The plan's own § 12 contains "Sample findings (illustrative, written as if from panel reviewing the current draft)." The brief pack methodology explicitly excluded the internal pre-review report to avoid biasing seats. Six worked-example findings inside the plan itself defeat that discipline at the source.

## Findings

### F-RH-001 [P0] [SCOPE]

**Plan section cited:** § 3 AC-1, § 4 Phase C, § 6 Reused-infrastructure table, § 6.5 D5
**Defect:** The plan adds `SkillVersion` as "the 14th canonical entity" in `intent-eval-core`. Blueprint B § 2.9 already defines `SkillSnapshot` as the immutable, SHA-pinned, content-addressed identity anchor for a skill, with `combined_sha`, indefinite retention, "Immutable. A SkillSnapshot is never modified after creation. A new snapshot for the same logical skill is a new row with a new UUID and a new `combined_sha`." That is precisely what § 4 Phase A's plan describes for `SkillVersion`: append-only, content-addressed, parent pointer, no in-place mutation. The plan's § 6 table line for SkillSnapshot reads verbatim: _"Phase C SkillVersion extends/references this (NOT a duplicate — SkillSnapshot pins source state; SkillVersion captures refinement lineage)."_ That parenthetical is the entire load-bearing defense — and it is asserted, not derived. The D5 ER diagram has them as two boxes with a `parent_hash` FK from SkillVersion to SkillSnapshot, but no row in either entity's schema is named that cannot already live on SkillSnapshot (a `parent_snapshot_id` field on a new SkillSnapshot row trivially captures refinement lineage; the existing SkillSnapshot is already "a new row with a new UUID for the same logical skill").
**Hickey-position invoked:** _"Don't repeat yourself applies to knowledge, not lines."_ The "knowledge" here is "what version of this skill was evaluated" — one concept. The plan instantiates it twice with overlapping schemas (both content-addressed, both immutable, both with parent pointers, both referenced by ScoreRecord).
**Why this matters:** The kernel is the ONE source-of-truth all five repos codegen from. Shipping two entities for one concept means every downstream consumer (audit-harness, j-rig, intent-rollout-gate, future partners) must learn the distinction, route correctly, and never confuse them. The "extends/references" framing in the table is exactly the kind of seam where consumers will drift. Worse, the plan ships this in `@intentsolutions/core@0.3.0` with sigstore provenance — once published the schema is on a one-way door (the same argument the plan uses for predicate URIs in § 4 Phase C).
**Proposed remediation:** Defect stated. The user decides whether SkillVersion is a distinct kernel entity, a new field on SkillSnapshot, or a separate predicate body that doesn't get its own entity row. Not the panel's job to choose.

### F-RH-002 [P0] [SCOPE]

**Plan section cited:** § 3 AC-12, § 3.5 PR-1, § 5.5 "Tri-linkage verification", § 6.5 D7
**Defect:** AC-12 mandates that every Refiner doc, bead, and GH issue carry the other two artifact IDs in its body as plaintext lines (`Beads:` / `Doc:` / `GitHub:` / `Bead:`). § 5.5 defines `validate-trilink.sh` to grep all three surfaces and fail CI if any of the three locations is missing the references to the other two. § 6.5 D7 enshrines the triangle in a normative diagram with the caption: _"any new artifact in the triangle must be born with the other two references already present."_ This is three plain-text copies of the same identity relationship, kept in agreement by a verifier rather than by derivation from a single source. There is exactly one piece of underlying knowledge ("these three artifacts are linked"); the plan stores it in three writable places and writes a sync engine + CI gate to keep them aligned.
**Hickey-position invoked:** _"Place-oriented programming is a smell."_ And separately: _"Don't repeat yourself applies to knowledge, not lines."_ A bead's `GitHub:` line, a GH issue's `Bead:` line, and a doc's `Beads:` header are three places holding the same fact: `bead X ↔ issue Y ↔ doc Z`. The plan even introduces `bd-sync` extension (§ 5.5) to _mirror_ the values — explicit acknowledgment that the three places will drift, with mirroring + a nightly verifier + a "self-healing" `gh issue edit --body-file` injection (§ 5.5 last subsection) as the mitigation stack.
**Why this matters:** The plan recognizes the drift risk (§ 8 risk row "per-repo bead clusters require label discipline that's easy to forget") and proposes more enforcement (CI gate, bd-sync extension, beads-guru agent). Every layer of mitigation is a tell. The relationship `bead-id ↔ doc-path ↔ gh-issue-number` is data; it could live in one place (the bead, which is already the SoT per the plan's own § 4 source-of-truth hierarchy: "bd wins for task state") and be projected into doc front-matter and GH issue bodies as a build artifact. The plan instead makes humans and agents responsible for hand-writing the same value in three places, then writes machinery to detect when they fail.
**Proposed remediation:** Defect stated. The user decides whether the triangle is one fact projected three ways, or three facts the verifier reconciles.

### F-RH-003 [P0] [SCOPE]

**Plan section cited:** § 12 "Sample findings (illustrative, written as if from panel reviewing the current draft)"; six worked examples F-CH-001, F-AK-001, F-LL-001, F-KB-001, F-MK-001, F-WC-001 inline
**Defect:** The plan-under-audit contains, inside its own § 12, six fully written sample findings attributed to seats by initial — Chip Huyen, Andrej Karpathy, Leslie Lamport, Kent Beck, Martin Kleppmann, Ward Cunningham. The README explicitly excluded the internal pre-Plan-Audit review report from the brief pack on the grounds that _"showing it would bias seats toward predetermined conclusions per the article-consistency seat's own P2 finding."_ Yet the brief pack DOES include this plan, and the plan contains pre-written findings for six of the seven seats, including specific proposed remediations ("Add Phase A.5 ...", "Insert Phase A.7 ...", "Add canonical-glossary entry: _Refinement(S, S', E) holds iff..._"). A seat reading the brief pack sees those before writing its own findings.
**Hickey-position invoked:** _"Simple is not easy."_ It is _easy_ to write sample findings — they make the framework feel concrete and demonstrate the format. It is not _simple_ — the audit framework now complects "what an audit deliverable looks like" (a format question) with "what defects are expected to be found" (an empirical question). One concept ("audit format") is doing the job of two ("audit format" + "pre-baked findings").
**Why this matters:** This finding is meta but expensive. The hard gate (§ 12 "No bd claim until STATUS.md reads RATIFIED") means the audit is the gating mechanism for $weeks-to-months of build work. If the panel is biased toward the six sample findings, the audit cannot do its job — which the audit framework itself says is _"to find what is wrong with the plan, not to improve the plan."_ And the plan's contents become self-fulfilling: F-CH-001 (eval-set bootstrap fallback), F-AK-001 (bitter-lesson baseline), F-LL-001 (define "refined"), F-KB-001 (bandwidth model), F-MK-001 (lineage durability), F-WC-001 (AAR cadence) — these are now things a faithful seat is primed to either ratify (= they found what was already in the plan) or to NOT raise (= they were already known, why repeat). Either way the audit's signal is degraded.
**Proposed remediation:** Defect stated. The user decides whether the worked examples should remain inline or be moved to a separate non-brief-pack appendix referenced only after Phase 2 review completes.

### F-RH-004 [P1] [GAPS]

**Plan section cited:** § 4 Phase A "Single mutable pointer file: `.j-rig/refiner/pointers/<skill>/best` (just a hash)"
**Defect:** The plan correctly identifies the append-only event log + content-addressed store as the value-oriented core (AC-2). It then immediately reintroduces mutation as a single file containing "just a hash" — the `pointers/<skill>/best` file. The plan does not specify: (a) the ordering semantics under concurrent refiner runs (two `bg refiner` invocations from the L2 Line hook in two parallel sessions both finishing within the same second); (b) what happens to a hash that was the value of `best` and is then superseded (the rejected-edit buffer is explicit in § 4 Phase E and § 6.5 D4, but a _previously-accepted-then-superseded_ SkillVersion is not the same thing as a rejected edit); (c) whether the pointer file's history is itself an append-only log somewhere or just gets overwritten on each promote; (d) reader consistency — what does a process see if it reads `pointers/<skill>/best` while a write is in flight.
**Hickey-position invoked:** _"Place-oriented programming is a smell."_ `pointers/<skill>/best` is precisely a place — a named slot that holds an identity, mutated by promotion. The hash is a value; the pointer is a place. The plan's value-oriented discipline applies cleanly to the SkillVersion store and the event log, then stops at the pointer.
**Why this matters:** This is exactly the boundary where value-oriented systems leak. The plan's whole brand is "evals-gated improvement loop, audit trail trusted" — the audit trail's primary trust artifact (§ 8 risk row "promotion → rollback → re-promotion cycles will corrupt audit trail") depends on knowing, for any point in time T, what the `best` pointer was pointing at. A bare mutable hash file with no specified ordering / history / consistency cannot answer that.
**Proposed remediation:** Defect stated. The user decides whether `best` should be a derived projection from the event log (= read the latest accept event for this skill) or a separately persisted append-only pointer log, and where the consistency guarantee comes from.

### F-RH-005 [P1] [SCOPE]

**Plan section cited:** § 3 AC-11 (revised 2026-05-27); § 4 Phase C SkillVersion schema fields `skill_md_open_spec` + `skill_md_cc_extension_version`
**Defect:** AC-11 splits the spec compliance into two layers — an open-standard layer (agentskills.io) and a Claude Code extension layer — and mandates that every SkillVersion record carry **both** version fields: `skill_md_open_spec: "agentskills.io/v1.0-<sha256-prefix>"` and `skill_md_cc_extension_version: "claude-code/2.1.152-<sha256-prefix>"`. The plan's motivation (§ 2.5) is that agentskills.io is a portable cross-vendor artifact (~32 tools); the Claude Code extensions are additive. But the data model now embeds an implementation detail of the spec authorities (Anthropic publishes a subset; the rest publishes another subset) into every value-record the kernel signs.
**Hickey-position invoked:** _"Complecting"_ — tangling concerns that should be independent. The concern "what skill content was validated" is one thing. The concern "which two upstream spec authorities published which fields at the time" is another. AC-11 binds them into the same record.
**Why this matters:** When agentskills.io v2 ships or Claude Code adds a third extension layer (or removes an existing one), every prior SkillVersion record carries fossilized version strings that the consumer must learn to ignore. The plan acknowledges drift management via "quarterly bd memory" (AC-11) but the drift goes into a signed value-record that the plan emits to sigstore Rekor (a one-way door per § 4 Phase C). A predicate URI body or a side-table that the SkillVersion references would isolate the volatile part from the durable part. Two version fields inside the SkillVersion row do not.
**Proposed remediation:** Defect stated. The user decides whether spec-version pinning belongs in the SkillVersion row, in the `skill-refiner-pass/v1` predicate body, or in a separate side table referenced by hash.

### F-RH-006 [P2] [SCOPE]

**Plan section cited:** § 4 Phase A CLI (`j-rig refine bootstrap|score|propose|apply|status`); § 4 Phase B CLI (`/j-rig refine bootstrap|propose|shadow|promote|status`)
**Defect:** The Refiner library exposes a five-command CLI (`bootstrap`, `score`, `propose`, `apply`, `status`). The Phase B plugin re-exposes a five-command CLI (`bootstrap`, `propose`, `shadow`, `promote`, `status`) — overlapping on `bootstrap`, `propose`, `status`; diverging on `score` / `apply` (library) vs `shadow` / `promote` (plugin). Two surfaces, partially overlapping vocabulary, no specification of why a user invokes `j-rig refine bootstrap` (library shell-out) vs `/j-rig refine bootstrap` (plugin command).
**Hickey-position invoked:** _"Complecting"_ — the same verb (`bootstrap`) is wired into two different command surfaces with different contracts. The user / consumer must learn the table of which command lives where.
**Why this matters:** Less expensive than the entity duplication (F-RH-001) but the same shape. One concept ("bootstrap an eval set for this skill") in two places. When the implementation evolves (e.g., `bootstrap` gains a flag), the maintainer must update two surfaces and decide whether they evolve together or diverge intentionally.
**Proposed remediation:** Defect stated. The user decides whether the plugin commands are pure passthroughs (one canonical surface, two invocation paths) or whether they are genuinely different operations and should have non-overlapping names.

### F-RH-007 [P2] [GAPS]

**Plan section cited:** § 4 Phase A `score(skillDoc, evalSet, modelTier?: 'haiku' | 'sonnet'): ScoreRecord`; § 3 AC-3 (multi-dimensional score records); § 4 Phase A illustrative `ScoreRecord = { behavioral: number; readability: number; [dimension: string]: unknown; }`
**Defect:** The `ScoreRecord` is declared as a value type but its specified shape is `{ behavioral, readability, [dimension: string]: unknown }`. The accept gate (AC-3 + § 4 Phase A's `accept(scoreV1, scoreV2): boolean`) is the load-bearing predicate of the whole product — the durable contribution per AC-7. Yet the plan never specifies: what predicate `accept` evaluates over an unbounded `[dimension: string]: unknown` map. "Strict improvement on all dimensions" works for a fixed set; it does not have a definition for an open record where dimensions can be added.
**Hickey-position invoked:** _"Simple is not easy."_ It is _easy_ to write `[dimension: string]: unknown` — it accommodates whatever a future caller wants. It is not _simple_ — the type does not have one shape, one role, one place. The accept predicate's behaviour is undefined for any caller who passes a `ScoreRecord` with a dimension the predicate's authors did not anticipate.
**Why this matters:** This is the same defect as F-LL-001's example finding (one of the plan's pre-baked samples) but at the type level rather than the glossary level. The plan asserts "strict improvement on all dimensions" without specifying the dimension set or how a new dimension joins it. Two engineers can implement two different accept predicates and both will type-check.
**Proposed remediation:** Defect stated. The user decides whether ScoreRecord has a closed dimension set or whether `accept` carries an explicit dimension-set parameter.

## Out-of-scope observations

- The plan codifies "Hickey-aligned" twice (AC-2 append-only, AC-3 multi-dim) and "Huyen-aligned" once (AC-4 shadow→canary→promote). The framing is courteous but the plan does not need attribution to defend the design choices on their own merits. Names risk turning architectural commitments into appeals to authority.
- § 4 Phase A `score()` is described as a "pure operation" in the type signature comment but the same paragraph declares it shells out to j-rig CLI which runs rollouts against an LLM. That is not a pure function; it is an effect inside an interface declaration. The plan is honest about this in D8 ("ADAPTERS live in @j-rig/refiner, NOT core") but the Phase A code comment in the snippet does not match.
- The "snoopy-fluttering-comet" codename appears in 8 places across the plan. Codenames are a brand-narrative tool, not an architectural one. They tend to outlive the plan they were chosen for and end up in commit messages, file names, and future onboarding docs. Worth re-considering once the plan reaches ratification — the artifact's content is what survives, not the codename.
